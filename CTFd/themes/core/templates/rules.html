DG Sentinels Protocol

CTF Rules

Play fair, stay in scope, and keep the competition safe for every operator.

Core Rules

  • Do not attack, disrupt, or interfere with the CTF infrastructure, challenge services, or other teams.
  • No denial-of-service, flooding, brute-force abuse, or scanning outside the intended challenge scope.
  • Do not share flags, exploits, hints, or full solutions with other teams or spectators.
  • Use only your own account and your own team workspace.
  • Any attempt to manipulate the scoreboard, accounts, submissions, or platform data is forbidden.
  • Use the challenge files, hints, endpoints, and provided tooling as intended by the challenge authors.
  • If a challenge seems broken, report it to the organizers instead of exploiting the issue for points.
  • Respect organizer decisions on disputes, invalid submissions, and rule violations.

Fair Play

  • Collaborate only within your own team.
  • Reverse engineering, web testing, cryptography, forensics, OSINT, and pwn are allowed only within the challenge boundaries.
  • Automated tooling is fine when it stays in scope and does not harm the platform.
  • Flags must be submitted exactly as provided by the challenge.
  • Keep findings private until the event ends unless organizers say otherwise.
